Italian

Being a prominent Romance language, Italian is largely derived from Vulgar Latin, a linkage that is made evident with the similarities in their verb conjugations (and various words in Italian resemble those in latin; what a surprise!). In terms of grammar, Italian is a relatively straightforward language, with consistent rules, though exceptions to general rules are occasional.

For instance, despite common classification rules, the word "hand" in Italian, or "mano," is a feminine noun that utilizes a definite article of "la".

It is also intriguing to note that Italian is an inflected language, meaning that a word's definition could be changed by altering their endings. When contrasted with English pronounciation, Italian pronounciation and spelling can be very easy to pick up: words are spelled like they sound, and letters are pronounced the way you expect them to be pronounced!
