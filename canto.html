Cantonese

Cantonese, commonly spoken in the southern regions of Canton, is a dialect of "Chinese." Although Cantonese and Mandarin share periodic vocabulary similiarities, the speakers of each dialect often are unable to comprehend the other dialect due to the differences in their pronunciations. Even within areas that speak Cantonese, regional dialects (Hong Kong Cantonese and Taishanese) add their own twist to the once dominating language of China.

The "Chinese" language is known for its tonal features, which means that the pitch contour of the voice while saying a syllable is a distinctive feature that differentiates words in the language, and homophones often further the reliance of Cantonese speakers on context.

Cantonese alone has nine different tones; there are six distinct pitch contours in Cantonese; an additional three are “entering tones.” By pronouncing the numbers 394052786 in Cantonese, all nine tones/pitches can be demonstrated.

While it depends on the region in which the dialect of Cantonese is spoken, native speakers often use the traditional Chinese writing system, which incorporates... well, traditional radicals and characters, distinguishing itself from the simplified system. Traditional characters typically involve more strokes, and some characters may include over 57 strokes. Pretty complicated, I know.
